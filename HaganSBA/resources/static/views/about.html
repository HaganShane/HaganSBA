History of The Masters

The Masters is one of the four major championships in professional golf. It is the first of the majors to be played each year, and it is always held at Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia, USA. Over four days and 72 holes, the smallest field in major championship golf competes for a chance to capture the Green Jacket and a place in Masters history. The event was originally founded in 1934 by Bobby Jones and Clifford Roberts.

Schedule

The Masters typically takes place in April. The exact dates for the tournament change each year, so be sure to check the official Masters website for the most up-to-date information. However, it generally takes place during the first or second week of April each year. The entire event spans over a week, and consists of different pre-tournament get togethers, competitions, and ceremonies. The actual tournament begins on the Thursday, with the final day being on the Sunday.

Tickets

Tickets for The Masters are highly sought after and can be difficult to obtain. The best way to purchase tickets is through the official Masters website or through authorized ticket resellers. There is an application process on the official Masters website that you can fill out, with a chance to be selected to purchase tickets. The selection is completely random, but after the selection is made, it is a first come, first serve general admission. The application process for the next year generally opens starting in the month of June.
