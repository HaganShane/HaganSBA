Overview of the Masters

The Masters is one of the four major championships in professional golf and is held annually in April at the Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia. It is a 72-hole stroke play tournament, with a field of approximately 90 players. The winner of the tournament receives a green jacket, which has become one of the most iconic prizes in all of sports. The tournament is known for its strict dress code, traditional menu, and its pristine course conditions. The Masters has a rich history and has been won by some of the greatest golfers of all time, including Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and Jordan Spieth.
